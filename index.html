Sir David Frederick Attenborough

Showing humans nature unseen

  • 1926 - On the 8th of May, David is born in Isleworth, Middlesex, England.
  • 1936 - Attenborough and his brother attend a lecture by Grey Owl which would later influence his conservation advocacy.
  • 1947 - National service calls upon David to serve two years in North Wales and Firth of Forth.
  • 1950 - Marries Jane Elizabeth Ebsworth Oriel, who he later has two children with, Robert and Susan.
  • 1952 - Joins the BBC full-time after his application was rejected two years prior.
  • 1956 - David becomes Controller of BBC Two, succesor of Michael Peacock.
  • 1972 - Having been promoted yet again three years prior, David was up for another promotion. He had no desire to be working administrative roles and wanted to be working in the wild. He leaves these roles to go on planning his natural history epics.
  • 1979 - "Life On Earth" begins and sets the benchmark of quality for all wildlife filmaking to come.
  • 2005 - "Wildlife On One", a series David had been narrating since it's beginning, had finished. One episode during it's air time "Meerkats United", was voted the best wildlife documentary for all time in BBC history.
  • 2006 - Attenborough and his brother Richard are made Distinguished Honorary Fellows of the University of Leicester.
  • 2006 - Finally breaks his silence on human influence of climate change. He states that a 2004 lecture had finally convinced him humans were responsible.
  • 2013 - David joins rock guitarists Brian May and Slash in opposing government policy on the cull of badgers in the UK.
  • 2020 - "David Attenborough: A Life on Our Planet" is released. The documentary is his witness statement and reflection on his career. It is his hopes for changing the future.
  • 2021 - Attends the 47th G7 Summit and states the immediate concerns on climate and environmental changes.
  • 2022 - Recieved the Champion of the Earth award from the United Nations Environment Programme, a most fitting accolade.
